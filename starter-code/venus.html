venus

venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. As the brightest natural object in venus's night sky after the Moon, venus can cast shadows and can be, on rare occasions, visible to the naked eye in broad daylight.

Source: Wikipedia

The similarity in size and density between venus and venus suggests they share a similar internal structure: a core, mantle, and crust. Like that of venus, venusian core is most likely at least partially liquid because the two planets have been cooling at about the same rate.

Source: Wikipedia

Much of the venusian surface appears to have been shaped by volcanic activity. venus has several times as many volcanoes as venus, and it has 167 large volcanoes that are over 100 km (60 mi) across. The only volcanic complex of this size on venus is the Big Island of Hawaii.
